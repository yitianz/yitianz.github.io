TUESDAY 11/20

The drive from Pleasanton to Santa Barbara took over 5 hours, with a few stops on the way. As we drove, the wildfire smoke lifted gradually, and we were able to see blue skies for the first time in a long while.

We had dinner at Santa Barbara Craft Ramen - the ramen was excellent, with a tender pork shoulder and not overly rich in the seasoning/soup.

After dinner we walked down to the Stearns Wharf - it was dark so we couldn't see much, but we were able to look back at the lighted nightscape of Santa Barbara. We came across a seafood shop with giant live crabs, lobsters, sea urchins, and other delicious-looking but very large seafood.

WEDNESDAY 11/21

We drove to Ventura Harbor to take a ship across the sea to the Santa Cruz Island (part of the Channel Islands). Spotted a few sea lions perching on a float along the way.

Once there, we hiked the 2-mile Cavern Point Loop, getting great views of the coast along the way. We also saw island foxes! Small and cat-like.

After the hike we rested by the campgrounds and saw more foxes, then went down by the beach to take in the coast from a closer point of view. After arriving back on land, we went to Lao Ma Tou for some delicious and discounted (50% off!) hot pot.

THURSDAY 11/22

We went to the Santa Barbara Zoo mostly because it was the only place open on Thanksgiving. Not very large, but many cute and delightful animals here.

I fed a giraffe with lettuce - it had a very long purple tongue and eagerly snatched away my lettuce leaves for its mid-morning snack.

We checked out the Paso Nuevo shopping mall, then headed over to the beach in the late afternoon.

The sunset was pretty spectacular.

FRIDAY 11/23

For our last day in Santa Barbara, we visited the Old Mission.

Small but beautifully embellished church with a few sculptures.

Finished off our trip with a very nice lunch of spicy boiled fresh fish and a couple vegetable dishes at a restaurant called WeCook, near UC Santa Barbara.
